Despite the numerous interventions to curb the endangers of HIV/AIDS, Ghana and the world at large should have taken the following necessary steps when HIV/AIDS was detected; Awareness. Ghana and the world at large should have intensified the awareness of the disease by educating the society through the media available by then such as radio, newspapers and television. The World Health Organisation (W.H.O) should have thrown more light on the causes, symptoms and effects on the overwhelming disease. There should have been the need for sensitization. Thus, HIV/AIDS should have been a subject of study in the basic schools to enlighten kids whiles they grow. The World Health Organisation could have organised seminars in local dialects of nations to create awareness to the illiterates. However, taking keen interest on mass screening should have been a measure the world Health Organisation (W.H.O) could have considered, after the case was identified to help detect infected persons from uninfected ones to enable them educate the infected on how to control the virus whiles the uninfected would be educated on how not to contract the virus due to lack of medication to subdue the virus. HIV/AIDS research includes all medical research that attempts to prevent, treat or cure HIV/AIDS as well as fundamental research about the nature of HIV as an infectious agent and AIDS as the disease caused by HIV. To this regard, individuals should have been encouraged to take career in HIV/AIDS due to the insufficient researchers of HIV/AIDS as at that time. World Health Organization (W.H.O) could have also funded the research towards HIV in order to have invented drugs in due time when the disease was detected. Again, most countries were economically unstable which necessitated “the operation feed yourself”. Due to that, individuals found themselves engaging in immoral works such as prostitution which to an extent spread the virus abroad. The W.H.O in collaboration with the World Bank could have come out with financial policies to help improve the economic stability of poverty stricken countries. They could have supported countries by establishing vocational institutions to help reduce immoral works like prostitution. In conclusion, the National Institute of Health (NIH) representing the largest public investment of HIV/AIDS research in the world is engaging in research around the globe to understand, diagnose, treat and prevent HIV/AIDS infection and its many associated conditions and to find cure, and also to encourage free screening and testing for HIV/AIDS in both private and government health agencies including pharmacies to provide preventive services , save lives and improve health conditions of carriers, since the stigma as to people being kicked out of their jobs, harassed and assaulted by community who found out their status and sometimes killed was a wide spread.
